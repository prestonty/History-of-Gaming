2010

GameBoy & GameBoy Color

The Gameboy was his first ever console and the size was perfect for him. The build is still holding up to this day in mint condition.

Had an iconic yellow GameBoy, passed down by his older cousin. There were two games - both of which were Pokemon.

Pokemon Blue

Pokemon Blue, originally released as Pocket Monsters Blue as a special edition in Japan, was brought to America in 1998.
This was the game he played, it "belonged" to him. Since it had a blastoise on the cartridge, he felt obligated to choose squirtle. He also liked the colour blue, and this established the unwritten rule of always choosing the water type starter.

He was incapable of reading/did not try to read, so he did not make it far. All of his run throughs ended when he constantly failed to navigate through a dark cave because he did not know the TM flash existed.

Pokemon Red

The cartridge was red, it had a big dragon on the front! Pokemon Red has one of the most iconic box arts in the franchise and sold over 31.38 million copies making it the franchise's best-selling video game.
This was the game his sibling played, it "belonged" to her. Charmander was the cooler starter and this later inspired him to use it on one of his play throughs (but he still got stuck in the cave).

2011

DS Lite

This was his baby, his everything, his brother, his best friend. He even named his console "Silver" due to its shiny grey exterior. When it got sick (got wet in the rain during camping), he would take care of it (put it in rice, heat it up in the mini fridge). When he returns from chinese school on Saturdays, it would be waiting for him to return so he can play Pokemon Black & White.

Games for the DS

Pokemon Diamond

Pokemon Diamond was by far his favourite Pokemon Game. It got him into buying handbooks from Indigo which led him down a rabbit hole of collecting one for the remaining Pokemon games for the DS release. He has spent the most amount of time on that game. At one point, he was stuck in a simulation of battling level 10 Bidoofs on route 201. When he regained conscious, he previously level 30 Prinplup grew up into a level 71 Empoleon (Not joking, the overleveled starter trope was real).
He also obtained darkrai, arceus, and shaymin by using the speedy bike to performing a area render error and by following a youtube video, he would walk in the void towards his favourite legendaries in the game.

Pokemon Black & White

One of the best stories in Pokemon, Black and White tells a story about more realistic antagonists. Team Plasma wants to liberate Pokemon and is led by a more relatable leader.
He felt cold towards this game because he had to learn the names of 156 new Pokemon, their types, and the new moves that actually do damage.

Pokemon White 2

Pokemon White 2 is so amazing considering it is a sequel to the first game. This was a risky move for Pokemon, trying to resell two sequel games in the same region again but it turned out to be extremely successful. The developers changed the towns which would have been reached by post game to the starting towns. This was a continuation to the previous game occurring two years after the fall of team Plasma. The amount of Pokemon was amazing and with this variety, he decided to choose Lucario again. This game was also gifted to him by his older cousin. Played two run throughs, transferred his pokemon for double xp shares.

Mario 64

The amount of creativity, and inspiration that went into each level was remarkable. There is no need for open world games when each level is this replayable. The music and atmosphere created by the birds chirping, the rabbits hopping around the bush maze, and the butterflies twirling out of the bushes creates a nostalgic experience he could never forget. This game was also the first video he watched from Dunkey and he is proud to be a Nintendo enjoyer. It actually started his love for mario and he originally thought there were only 64 stars (he was wrong). Definitely in the top 3 for favourite DS Games.

Metroid Prime Hunters

This was his first horror game. It was very dark and isolated. This is why he only played co-op with his sibling or versus battles. Star Wars III: The Clone Wars

Amazing game, the cut scenes had no dialogue or text so he enjoyed it. Almost certain he 100% the game with his sibling through co-op. He really liked playing with his sibling and learned the lore of Star Wars thanks to this.

Super Princess Peach

Most underrated game. It was a very strange platformer, utilizing emotions as temporary power-ups and having an energy bar. He would often spam the power-ups and have to restart the level constantly because he used all his energy points.

Nintendogs

He loved his chichiwawa which he named Rex. This name actually originated from his older cousin's dog, the same person who passed down his heirloom (The Gameboy). One day, after Preston and his sibling finished Saturday School at Marilake Education Centre, they stayed at their grandmother's house. Their cousin had to leave and left a dog in their care. They wanted to play with the dog so badly but their mother made sure they would finish the homework they got from Math and English class first (IT WAS SIX PAGES PER COURSE). He had a blurry memory which most certainly was a dream where he and his sibling would enjoy a meal with the dog. In other words they ate dog food with the dog... 7/10
Although Saturday school took away a few hours from spending time with a real dog, he most confess, Marilake was what built his exam writing skills, his math skills, and showed him how talented the other asian kids from different cities could be. It truly picked him up from his laziness and became a better student because of it.
Anyways, Nintendogs was the upgraded version of Tamagotchi, allowing you to take walks with your dog, feed them, shower them, play frisbee with them, and train them to speed through an obstacle course for dogs to win competitions.

2017

Nintendo Switch

On his vacation to Hong Kong in the summer of 2017, he saw advertisements of the Nintendo Switch. He got his last console, the 3DS, many years ago and forgot about the world of gaming, he was mainly an fan of watching games.
This all changed when he visited Sham Shui Po Station. This was where all the underground tech stores were located and he was first attracted by the retro DS games on the display shelves. He realized Nintendo was a big part of the gaming industry and was making a comeback with their new Nintendo Switch. It was portable, just like his DS', but had the capabilities of the Wii by having the properties of a home console.
After he begged his mom, they agreed to get a discounted package: A Nintendo Switch with a fancy blue case, along with 3 games - Arms, Splatoon 2, and The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild. At that night back at the hotel, he played his first third-person shooter - Splatoon.
